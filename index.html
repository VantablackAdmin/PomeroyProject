The Story Behind the Lantern

A Town Built on One Street and a Thousand Memories

Pomeroy is unlike any town on the Ohio. They call it the Skinny City — nearly a mile of 19th-century storefronts pressed shoulder to shoulder against the hillside, with the river lapping at the doorsteps across the way. There was never room to spread out. So the town simply stacked its history on top of itself, year after year, flood after flood.

And when a place holds that much memory in so narrow a space, some of it refuses to leave. Behind the ornate brick facades and the tarnished gold lettering, residents have whispered for generations about footsteps on empty stairs, lanterns that move with no hand to carry them, and a woman in mourning black who walks the waterline at dusk.

Your guide carries an oil lantern and a head full of these stories — the documented, the disputed, and the ones the old families will only tell after dark. This is not a haunted house. This is a real town, and these are its real ghosts.

Stops Along the Walk

The Haunts of the Skinny City

Six stops. One lantern. A mile of the most storied street in southern Ohio.

⚖️Photo: the old courthouse
Stop One

The Courthouse Clock

One of Ohio's oldest courthouses, raised in 1848. They say the clock still chimes a thirteenth hour on the coldest nights — and that someone is always waiting on the steps.

🏚️Photo: the mansion
Stop Two

The Hillside Mansion

A grand home above the river, restored over decades by a family who swears they were never quite alone. Shadows in the windows. Footsteps in the attic. And something watching from the trees.

⚓Photo: the waterfront
Stop Three

The Woman at the Waterline

Where the floods came again and again, a figure in mourning black is seen at dusk, searching the muddy bank for something the river took and never gave back.

🍺Photo: old storefront / tavern
Stop Four

The Back-Room Tavern

A century-old storefront that ran liquor when liquor was forbidden. Glasses still slide across the bar on their own, and the cellar door won't stay shut for anyone.

⛪Photo: cemetery / hilltop
Stop Five

The Lost Hilltop Graves

A cemetery forgotten by all but the oldest residents, swallowed by the hillside above town. The names have worn away. The visitors, it seems, have not.
